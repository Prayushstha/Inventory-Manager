What We Offer

Everything your business needs,
in one place.

Inventory Manager is built for small to medium business owners who want control over their inventory without the complexity. Here's what it does.

Generate Reports

Automatically generate detailed sales, stock, and performance reports. Export them as PDF or CSV to share with your team or accountant.

Stock Tracking

Monitor your inventory in real time. Get alerts when stock runs low, track quantities across categories, and never run out unexpectedly again.

Sales Records

Keep a clean log of every sale made. Filter by date, product, or amount to understand your revenue trends and best-selling items.

Customer History

View a complete purchase history for each customer. Build stronger relationships by knowing what they buy, how often, and how much they spend.

Product Catalog

A dedicated page for every product in your shop — wholesale price, retail price, product descriptions, methods of use, and more. Browse your full catalog at a glance or search for any item instantly.

Barcode Scanning

Scan product barcodes to instantly pull up item details, update stock counts, or process a sale — fast, accurate, and no manual typing needed.

How to use Inventory Manager

A quick guide to get you up and running. Full documentation coming soon.

01

Setting Up Your Account

Launch the app and create your business profile. Enter your shop name, contact details, and preferred currency. This information will appear across your reports and invoices.

02

Adding Products

Navigate to the Product Catalog and click "Add Product". Fill in the product name, description, barcode, wholesale price, retail price, and method of use. You can also bulk import products via CSV.

03

Managing Stock

Head to the Inventory section to view current stock levels. Set minimum stock thresholds for each product — Inventory Manager will alert you when items fall below that level so you can restock in time.

04

Recording a Sale

Use the Sales page to log each transaction. Scan a barcode or search by product name, select the quantity sold, and confirm. The system updates stock automatically and saves the record to customer history.

05

Generating Reports

Go to Reports and choose a date range and report type — Sales Summary, Stock Level, or Supplier Orders. Preview the report on screen or export it as a PDF or CSV file.

06

Managing Suppliers

Add your suppliers under the Suppliers tab. Store their name, contact, and the products they supply. When you need to restock, create a purchase order directly from the supplier's profile page.
